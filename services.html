Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the questions Kerala clients ask most often.

Will you guarantee that I get a visa or job?
?
No — and anyone who does is lying to you. Visa approvals are decided by the Government of Canada, IRCC, or other immigration authorities — not by any consultant. Job offers are made by employers. We provide honest guidance, professional documents, and real support — but we cannot and will not make false promises. This is clearly stated in our Service Agreement.
Can I pay in instalments?
Yes, absolutely. We understand that fees can be a challenge. A small advance deposit confirms your booking and work begins. The remaining balance is paid in agreed instalments as work progresses. The payment schedule is written into your Service Agreement so everything is clear from the start. We accept Google Pay, PhonePe, UPI, and Bank Transfer.
What if my application is rejected — do I get a refund?
No refund is given for visa or job rejections — because we provide guidance services, not outcome guarantees. However, if we fail to deliver agreed documents or services without valid reason, we will discuss a fair refund. This is clearly explained in our Service Agreement before you pay anything. We encourage you to read it carefully.
Can the price be reduced? It seems expensive.
Yes — just ask honestly. Every situation is different. We have Basic, Standard, and Complete tiers for every service. If the price is beyond your budget, tell me and we will find a tier that works. I would rather help you at a lower rate than see you go to a fake agency that takes ₹50,000 and disappears. Student discounts and family referral discounts also available.
Are you a licensed immigration agent or lawyer?
No — and I am completely transparent about this. I am an experienced career professional who personally navigated the Canadian AIP immigration process. I provide guidance, document preparation, and support based on real experience and official government information. For complex legal matters, I will always recommend a registered immigration consultant (RCIC) or lawyer. Honesty first — always.
Why do I need to sign an agreement?
For YOUR protection as much as ours. The agreement clearly states what services are included, the exact fee agreed, the payment schedule, and what happens in any dispute. It means there can be no confusion, no "you promised me more" situations, and no unexpected charges. Download it, read it carefully, and only sign when you are completely comfortable. This is standard practice for any professional service.
How long does the process take?
It depends on the service: CV and cover letters are delivered within 3–5 working days. Immigration guidance is ongoing. For Canada AIP, the full process from CV to job offer typically takes 3–12 months depending on your profile, sector, and employer response. University admissions timelines vary by intake. I will give you a realistic timeline during your free consultation — not an unrealistic promise.

Yes — 100%. I am based in Kollam, Kerala myself! All consultations are via WhatsApp call or video call. All documents are delivered digitally.
